Quality Of Life Analysis in Breast Cancer Patients Using Excel

This project evaluates health-related quality of life (HRQoL) and chemotherapy-related adverse effects in breast cancer patients, using the EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-BR23 instruments to gather data from 70 patients in tertiary care hospitals. Key findings include a higher breast cancer prevalence among premenopausal women aged 40–49, with nausea, alopecia, weight loss, and insomnia as common adverse effects; 71.1% of these effects are classified as "possible" and 28.9% as "probable" based on Naranjo’s causality assessment. Additionally, preventable adverse effects, such as nausea and constipation, emphasize the need for targeted interventions, particularly for younger women, who reported lower functional scores than older age groups.

Racial disparities of Covid Vaccination by County

This project examines the socioeconomic and political factors associated with racial disparities in COVID-19 vaccination rates across different states, using various data collection and analysis methods. The repository includes Python code for gathering and cleaning county-level vaccination data by race, implementing main regression analyses, and performing robustness checks to validate findings across different operationalizations and control variables. Additional analyses investigate age group disparities, vaccination rates at multiple time points, residential mobility effects, and model adjustments to address multicollinearity and sample-specific vaccination rates.
